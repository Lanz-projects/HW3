General Information about

Japan is an island nation located in East Asia, known for its rich cultural heritage, advanced technology, and stunning natural landscapes. Comprising four main islands—Honshu, Hokkaido, Kyushu, and Shikoku—along with thousands of smaller ones, Japan offers a unique blend of modernity and tradition. Its bustling cities, like Tokyo and Osaka, are centers of innovation, while its historic temples, serene gardens, and scenic mountains, such as Mount Fuji, reflect a deep cultural history. Japan is also famous for its cuisine, including sushi, ramen, and tempura, and its polite, respectful society.

Featured Events

  • Gion Matsuri: The Gion Matsuri dates from 869 and is the country's biggest festival. The festival turns the city into a huge party, especially during the two periods in mid-July when elaborately-decorated multi-ton floats are displayed and then pulled through the streets.
  • Sapporo Snow festival:: Using one of Sapporo's most abundant natural resources and their imaginations, a few students fashioned several snow sculptures in Odori Park way back in 1950. Fast-forward to today, and the Sapporo Snow Festival is a wintertime extravaganza that draws millions every year. Visit for a taste of Hokkaido hospitality, great food and drink, and stunning illuminations.
  • Hirosaki Cherry Blossom Festival: From late April to Early May, Hirosaki Park in Aomori Prefecture has some of Japan's best cherry blossoms. Impressive in scale and picturesque in setting, the Hirosaki Park cherry blossoms are one of Japan's top locations for hanami, or cherry blossom viewing.
  • Tokyo International Film Festival: The Tokyo International Film Festival has been honoring works by celebrated filmmakers from around the world since 1985. Held every October in Tokyo's central Hibiya, Ginza, and Yurakucho areas , the event is a major draw for top filmmakers and celebrities and is a glowing testament to Tokyo's cultural status.

Fun Facts

  1. Japan is made up of over 6,800 islands.
  2. Sumo wrestling is Japan's national sport.
  3. Mount Fuji is the country's tallest peak.
  4. Japanese people have one of the highest life expectancies in the world.
  5. Japan is known for its speedy bullet trains.
  6. It's home to the busiest pedestrian crossing in the world.
  7. It's polite to slurp your noodles.
  8. The number four is considered unlucky.
  9. Hanami is the tradition of cherry blossom viewing.
  10. There are thousands of onsen (hotsprings) throughout the country.

If you are wondering what the Japan National Tourism Organization (JNTO) does, it's quite simple. They:

  1. Promote Japanese tourism
  2. Operate the Tourist Information Center in Japan for international visitors
  3. Administer Guide-interpreter examinations
  4. Publish tourism statistics and market reports
  5. Provide support for international conventions and incentive events
